Organization Description: 
Blue Bees is a rehabilitation organization that uses the art of beekeeping to help recovering 
addicts and their children rebuild their lives. Founded on World Bee Day in 2022 by siblings 
Nuren and Melissa Mayer, Blue Bees was born from their own childhood experiences with 
addiction and the healing power of bees. Beekeeping teaches patience, care, and 
mindfulness — qualities that restore discipline and self-worth. For children, it offers a safe 
environment to learn responsibility, teamwork, and environmental stewardship. With the 
support of sponsors like The Honey Company, Brother Bees, and Best Bees Company, Blue 
Bees has helped over 270 recovering addicts and nearly 200 children rediscover stability, 
safety, and purpose. Our mission is simple: to bring light, warmth, and community to families 
affected by addiction. 
Reasons for creating the business 
I chose to create an organization and named it Blue Bees because beekeeping offers a 
unique kind of therapy. Working with bees needs patience, care, and mindfulness. These 
qualities help recovering addicts’ discipline and self-worth. For children of addicts, I think 
beekeeping provides a safe, proper environment where they can learn to be responsible, 
work in a team, and provide environmental care.
